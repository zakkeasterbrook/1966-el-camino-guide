1966 El Camino

The third model year of the second-generation El Camino blended Chevelle SS styling with truck utility. This page collects specs, option codes, market tips, and restoration notes so you can obsess over one of the coolest half-ton haulers ever built.

Factory specs Buying guide Timeline

Quick overview

  • Platform: Chevelle A-body (116-inch wheelbase)
  • Production: ~35,000 units for 1966 model year
  • Assembly plants: Atlanta, Baltimore, Fremont, Kansas City, Los Angeles
  • Body style: Two-door coupe utility, 1,500 lb payload

1966 introduced the Coke-bottle profile shared with Chevelle SS396 models, stacked headlights, and sculpted rear quarters. Inside you could spec strato-bucket seats with a center console or stick with the standard "taxi" bench. Drivetrains ranged from the humble 194ci six to the tire-frying L78 375 hp 396.

Factory powertrains & key specs

Inline sixes

  • 194ci (120 hp) — base engine, single-barrel
  • 230ci (140 hp) — optional, smoother torque

Small-block V8s

  • 283ci (195–220 hp) — 2-bbl or 4-bbl
  • 327ci (275–300 hp) — L30/L79 packages

Big-block V8s

  • 396ci L35 (325 hp) — hydraulic cam, broad torque
  • 396ci L34 (360 hp) — higher compression, 4-bbl Holley
  • 396ci L78 (375 hp) — solid lifter, 11:1 compression, 6,000 rpm screamer

Transmissions: 3-speed manual (column), 4-speed M20/M21 Hurst, 2-speed Powerglide, or the new-for-66 Turbo Hydra-Matic.

Suspension: Coil springs all around with boxed rear control arms on heavy-duty packages.

Brakes: 9.5" drums standard; metallic linings & power assist optional (front discs not until 1967).

Trim levels: Standard El Camino, Custom (Z16 appearance bits), and SS396 appearance package (RPO Z25).

Model year highlights

1964

Second-gen El Camino debuts on new Chevelle chassis.

396 big-block becomes available mid-year in Chevelle SS; limited run in El Camino.

1966

Buying & restoration notes

  1. Check frame rails & bed floor. Boxed rear sections trap moisture; look for pitting around the rear body mounts.
  2. Verify SS claims. RPO Z25 is appearance-only; real 396 powertrains will have matching engine suffix codes and heavy-duty suspension bits.
  3. Interior trim scarcity. Custom door panels and console pieces are 1-year-only; budget for reproduction parts.
  4. Big-block cooling. Upgrade to 4-core radiator and clutch fan if you’re running modern traffic speeds.
  5. Modern upgrades. Subtle disc brake conversions and overdrive automatics keep the truck usable without destroying value.
